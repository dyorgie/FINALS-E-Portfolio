Media and Information Literacy

Blog Article

Media literacy is the ability to recognize, analyse, decode, comprehend and understand different forms of media present. Since media comes in different forms, media literacy allows people to fully understand it regardless of form, and be able to form their own opinion and idea if needed.

Information literacy is the ability to find, analyse, organise, utilise, and communicate different types of information, mainly used for decision-making, problem-solving and research. There are different thinking skills involved, mainly critical and problem-solving skills that makes a person literate in handling information.

Technology literacy is the ability of a person to effectively use technology in all of its forms. Whether they are working independently or in collaboration, to be literate in technology they must be able to handle it responsibly and appropriately to communicate, manage, access, etc. information.

Media, information, and technology literacy affects society in a number of ways. First, the media. While media has been present for a long time now, it has been much more apparent in recent years, so much so that the youth is exposed to it at a much younger age compared to their parents. Now, with it being more and more present in our daily lives, everyone must be literate with media as it has a lot of influence. Journalism can be considered as a media, and we all know how important journalism is, but what is dangerous is if we do not understand or do not know how to use the information we have been presented. Which also ties into information literacy. While we know that journalism presents facts, we cannot deny that there is some bias present, and we need to analyse the data we have read ourselves. We know journalism can shape the opinions and ideas of the masses so it’s not good to just accept it for what it is. Another way how information literacy affects our society is through social media. Besides fake news itself which we need to look out for as well, people are often misunderstood in social media, mainly because most of the things are just seen on a screen or read by other users instead of it being actually heard in a normal conversation. I think if we truly possess information literacy, we would be able to widen our perspective regarding the things we see on social media, and always have a mindset that there is always something more to it than just what we see. Lastly, technology literacy. Just like the media, where it has become apparent in recent years, we cannot deny the fact that technology has basically integrated itself in our daily lives, and it won’t be long until we actually can’t live without it. Just right now, when the world is under a global pandemic, technology has been such a great help that if we were to remove it, a lot of things would be different. Technology affects our society in both good and bad ways, so it is up to us to be literate and be able to use it responsibly and for the better.
